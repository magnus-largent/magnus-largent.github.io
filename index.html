Searching for pulsars

Trottier Space Institute, McGill University, Montreal
During my M.Sc., I was a member of the CHIME All-Sky Multi-Day Pulsar Stack Search (CHAMPSS), which is searching for pulsars in the northern sky by using a novel method of power spectra summation. I created a crucial piece of our survey’s data pipeline; an algorithm to confirm and verify pulsar candidates. My algorithm confirmed the very first pulsars found using CHAMPSS, with 12 new pulsars confirmed to date.

Dark and Quiet Skies

I am interested in advocacy and outreach in the field of “dark and quiet skies”, which refers to mitigating radio and light pollution. The rapid development of cost-effective space launch systems in the past few years has led to an astounding expansion in the number of satellites, including so called ‘mega-constellation’ communication satellites.

These satellites have had a widespread impact on both amateur, professional, and cultural astronomy. It is estimated that in the near future, at certain latitudes during twilight, hundreds of satellites will be visible with the naked eye. Satellites can leave streaks of light that contaminate optical images and contribute to an overall brightening of the night sky due to light reflecting off of them. There are also severe impacts on radio astronomy, since the transmission power of satellites is often several magnitudes stronger than the astrophysical source being observed, resulting in contaminated observations. Satellites have even been observed to release unintended electromagnetic radiation in the observation bands of radio telescopes , despite these bands being protected by international regulations. Ground-based sources of radio frequency interference, like cellphones and television, can be mitigated by designating radio quiet zones away from populated areas, but satellites cannot strictly be avoided in the same way.

Many international bodies have emphasized the pressing nature of this subject, with the United Nations adding ‘Dark and Quiet Skies’ to the agenda for the Committee on Peaceful Uses of Outer Space last year and the International Astronomical Union even founding a Centre for the Protection of the Dark and Quiet Sky from Satellite Constellation Interference (IAU CPS) in 2022
